# Xiaochuan Li

MSc Human & AI · Causal NLP · Mechanistic Interpretability · Stable Inference

I study how language models preserve or fail to preserve stable reasoning mechanisms under perturbation, distribution shift, and intervention.

My current research direction is built around a simple idea:

> Reasoning is not just producing correct answers.  
> Reasoning is the preservation of stable mechanisms under perturbation and intervention.

I am especially interested in cases where models appear competent under standard evaluation, but fail when shortcut correlations, prompt framing, or environmental conditions change.

---

## Research Focus

- Stable inference under perturbation
- Shortcut learning and reasoning failure
- Mechanism shift in language models
- Controlled reasoning environments
- Latent control states and prompt framing
- Causal Inferential Yield (CIY)

## Research Philosophy

I prefer small, controlled experiments that make mechanisms visible over large benchmark results that are difficult to interpret.

My work treats perturbations, failures, and interventions not just as evaluation tools, but as instruments for understanding how model behavior is produced.

The long-term goal is to develop evaluation methods that distinguish stable mechanism-level reasoning from shortcut-based statistical behavior.
