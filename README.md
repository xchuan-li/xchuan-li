# Xiaochuan Li

**M.Sc. Human and AI · University of Technology Nuremberg (UTN)**

I do **causal interpretability through linguistic structure** — testing whether a model's stable, correct behavior is licensed by the genuine structure of a sentence (the head that governs agreement, the dependency that carries an inference), or by a surface cue that merely correlates. I run that question through typologically diverse languages, where the same meaning is encoded by morphology, word order, or context.

🌐 **[xchuan-li.github.io](https://xchuan-li.github.io)** — *Structure, or surface? Language is where you can tell.*

---

## Research — one program, through linguistic structure

A method to **certify** whether behavior is licensed by genuine structure, to **localize** where that structure is used, and to **quantify** how much of it the model relies on — then carried to the language frontier. The status on each line is where the work actually stands, not where it is headed.

| | Step | Project | Status |
|--:|---|---|---|
| **01** | Certify | [**Stable Is Not Grounded**](https://github.com/xchuan-li/stable-is-not-grounded) — stable-correct behavior isn't grounded unless the structure survives shortcut-severing audits | `drafting` |
| **02** | Localize | **Isotrace** — which cue (morphology, syntax, context) the model's reasoning actually leans on | `in design` · 🔒 |
| **03** | Quantify | **Causal Inferential Yield** — how much genuine structure the model reliably uses, beyond surface cues | `planned` · 🔒 |
| **↘** | Frontier | **Typological Grounding** — the same meaning re-encoded across grammars; does structural dependence transfer? | `exploratory` |

**Support — validation & extensions**
